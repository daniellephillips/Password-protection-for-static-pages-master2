Research Blurb

This research was a crucial piece of a larger initiative exploring AI-powered workloads, aimed at refining and optimizing performance analysis tools. Conducted over several months, this extensive study brought together internal stakeholders and an external research firm to ensure an unbiased, third-party perspective. The process began with internal stakeholder interviews to validate existing assumptions, followed by qualitative user interviews that compared the experiences of NVIDIA Nsight and Intel VTune Profiler users. This competitive analysis revealed deeper insights into how AI developers optimize workloads, their pain points, and what they truly need from performance profiling tools.

Key findings highlighted the growing demand for AI-driven recommendations, simplified interfaces, and smart guidance to streamline optimization. The study's conclusions informed actionable next steps, guiding future research and product improvements. Deliverables included a detailed research plan, discussion guide, participant interview recordings, and a synthesized analysis of key patterns and opportunities. This body of work ultimately influenced strategic decisions and product enhancements, reinforcing the importance of user-centered research in AI workload optimization.

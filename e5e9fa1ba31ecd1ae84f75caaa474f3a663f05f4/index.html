AI Strategy & Competitive Analysis
Intel Optimization and Analysis Tools

January – March 2024

For this research project with Intel on AI workload optimization, we used a structured, mixed-methods approach to identify gaps in a competitors AI capabilities and uncover opportunities for product design and innovation. Additionally, we cross-checked the knowledge, assumptions and ideas of our internal stakeholders to, validating data points.

Incluced in this research are: Competitor Analysis – Understanding power users of a competitor’s AI product to identify opportunities and weaknesses. Mixed-Methods Research – Using qualitative research, stakeholder interviews, and external validation to get a full picture. Strategic Alignment – Ensuring that findings from user research connect with business, engineering, and hardware priorities and answers questions relevant tpo moving forward. Tooling & Analysis – Using Dovetail to organize and extract insights, highlighting a structured and systematic research process. Ensuring the process is focused, structured, collaborative, visible and transparent, to all internal stakeholders . Actionable Outcomes – Insights feeding directly into product strategy, specifically around AI-driven recommendations. If we move to next steps, user stories are created and go straight to product planning. Scalability of Approach – Indicating that this framework can be applied to other areas like internal creative workflows or broader AI integrations. Overall, this is about validating AI optimization opportunities through structured UX research and ensuring that the findings directly shape product and strategy decisions. Sounds like a well-executed study with clear business impact.

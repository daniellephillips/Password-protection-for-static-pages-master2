AI Strategy & Competitive Analysis
Intel Optimization and Analysis Tools

January – March 2024

For this research project with Intel on AI workload optimization, we used a structured, mixed-methods approach to gather deep insights from power users of our competitor’s product. Our primary goal was to identify gaps in their AI capabilities and uncover opportunities for optimization. We combined qualitative user research, internal stakeholder interviews, and external validation to ensure a comprehensive understanding. A third-party firm conducted unbiased user interviews with target developers, while we engaged key internal teams—including business, engineering, and hardware-focused stakeholders—to align on priorities and validate assumptions. This collaborative approach helped bridge user needs with internal strategic goals.

All research data was organized in Dovetail, where we analyzed patterns and extracted actionable insights. Since we already had a solid grasp of our users' workflows, this study focused on validation and next steps. The outcome directly informed product strategy, leading to AI-driven recommendations for our design and development teams. This structured approach—internal coordination plus external validation—can be applied to other areas, such as studying internal creative workflows and exploring AI integration opportunities. The key is aligning internal goals, capturing user insights, validating findings, and translating them into actionable recommendations.
